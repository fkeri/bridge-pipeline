Most Recent Stochastic Background Results

Two plots will appear below, both with the angular separation between the pulsars on the horizontal axis. The upper plot is the cross-correlated power for the selected data sets. Please see Chamberlin et al. (2014) for more information. The lower plot is a histogram of the pulsar pairs (red) and the pulsar pairs weighted by their cross-correlation value (blue) as a function of angular separation, respectively. The two numbers that are displayed below the table are the most likely amplitude of the stochastic gravitational wave background and the signal-to-noise ratio of that signal.
